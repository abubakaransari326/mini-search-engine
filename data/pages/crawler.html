Web Crawler Basics

A crawler starts from seed pages and follows hyperlinks to discover additional pages. In a real deployment, politeness policies, rate limits, and robots exclusion standards matter. Here the focus is the core loop: fetch, parse, extract links, enqueue unvisited targets, repeat until the frontier is empty or a budget is reached.

For this mini site, links are local file references between HTML documents. That keeps the demo deterministic and avoids network variability while still exercising real hyperlink parsing.

The crawler should avoid revisiting a page that was already processed. A hash set of canonical document names is enough for this tiny corpus. In larger systems you normalize URLs carefully to prevent duplicates that differ only by trivial syntax.

Crawling also interacts with indexing: once text is extracted, tokenization and stop word removal feed the inverted index builder. The mineral name obsidian appears only on this page so you can combine it in queries with other unique tokens to force an empty conjunction result.
